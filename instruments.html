Basic Types of Instruments

Instruments come in all shapes and sizes. Some require you to blow into it while others need you to pluck a string. In all cases they make the most beautiful sounds that are all around us. Instruments come in 4 different groups: woodwinds, brass, percussion, and strings. Let's get more into depth on these instruments.

Woodwind

Woodwind instruments produce music by players either blowing air through them or vibrating a reed. Used across various musical genres, woodwinds contribute unique qualities to music. Hover over the instruments to see what sounds they make.

Flute Oboe Clarinet Bassoon

Brass

Brass instruments produce sound through the vibrations of players' lips against a mouthpiece. Brass instruments add a powerful and dynamic presence to musical compositions across genres. Hover over the instruments to see what sounds they make.

Trombone Trumpet Tuba French horn

Percussion

Percussion instruments create rhythmic patterns by being struck or shaken. Percussion adds energy and texture to music. Hover over the instruments to see what sounds they make.

Gong Xylophone Bass Drum Snare Drum

String

String instruments produce music by vibrating strings that are either plucked, bowed, or strummed. String instruments contribute both harmonic depth and emotive qualities to music. Hover over the instruments to see what sounds they make.

Harp Violin Guitar Bass
